AP Calculus: Optimization

Introduction to Optimization

Optimization problems are one of the most powerful real-world applications of differential calculus. Whether a manufacturer wants to minimize the cost of producing a cylindrical can, or a farmer wants to maximize the grazing area of a fenced field, the mathematical goal remains the same: finding the absolute extrema (maximum or minimum) of a function over a specific domain.

By finding where the derivative of our function equals zero or is undefined (our critical points), we can pinpoint the exact values that optimize our scenario.

Steps For Optimization:
  1. Draw a diagram and label all known and unknown variables.
  2. Identify the primary equation (the quantity to be maximized or minimized).
  3. Identify the secondary equation (the constraint that links your variables).
  4. Substitute the constraint into the primary equation to express it in terms of a single variable.
  5. Determine the feasible domain (what are the practical limits on your variable?).
  6. Differentiate the primary equation and find the critical points (where $f'(x) = 0$ or is undefined).
  7. Justify that your critical point is indeed the absolute extremum using the First Derivative Test, Second Derivative Test, or the Extreme Value Theorem (checking endpoints).
